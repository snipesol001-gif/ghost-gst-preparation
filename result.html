📝 Quick Revision Notes

  • Amalgamation (1914): Lord Lugard merged Northern & Southern protectorates for administrative and economic convenience, not cultural unity.
  • Indirect Rule: Successful in the North (Centralized Emirs), failed in the East (Acephalous/Igbo lacked kings, leading to the invention of Warrant Chiefs).
  • Constitutions: Clifford (1922) introduced Elective Principle; Richards (1946) entrenched Regionalism; Lyttelton (1954) established true Federalism.
  • Political Parties: NPC (North/Conservative), AG (West/Progressive), NCNC (East/Nationalist).
  • Kinship: Consanguinea (blood), Affinity (marriage). Primary (parents/siblings), Secondary (uncles/aunts), Tertiary (cousins-in-law).
  • Marriage Rules: Endogamy (marrying within group), Exogamy (marrying outside group).
  • Origins: Yoruba (Oduduwa/Ile-Ife), Bini (Ogiso era -> Eweka I), Hausa (Bayajida), Igbo (Acephalous/Direct Democracy via Amala Oha).
