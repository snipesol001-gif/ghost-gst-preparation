📝 Quick Cram Notes

  • Amalgamation (1914): Lord Lugard merged Northern & Southern protectorates for administrative/economic convenience.
  • Indirect Rule: Successful in North (Centralized Emirs), failed in East (Acephalous/Igbo lacked kings -> Warrant Chiefs invented).
  • Constitutions: Clifford (1922) = Elective Principle; Richards (1946) = Regionalism; Lyttelton (1954) = True Federalism.
  • Political Parties: NPC (North/Conservative), AG (West/Progressive), NCNC (East/Nationalist). NEPU represented Talakawa.
  • Kinship: Consanguinea (blood), Affinity (marriage). Primary (parents), Secondary (uncles), Tertiary (cousins-in-law).
  • Marriage Rules: Endogamy (marry within group), Exogamy (marry outside group). Polyandry = 1 woman, multiple men.
  • Origins: Yoruba (Oduduwa/Ile-Ife), Bini (Ogiso -> Eweka I), Hausa (Bayajida), Igbo (Acephalous/Amala Oha assembly).
  • Kanem-Bornu: Saifawa dynasty. Council of 12 = Nokena. Chief Judge = Maini Kanendi.
